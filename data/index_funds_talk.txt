Hey everyone, in this video I want to explain index funds in the simplest way I can. An index fund is a single investment that buys a whole basket of stocks tracking a market index, instead of trying to pick individual winners. The big advantage of an index fund is that it gives you broad diversification and very low fees at the same time. Because the fund just tracks the index, there is no expensive team of managers picking stocks, so the costs stay tiny. Over the long run, low fees and broad diversification are the two factors that matter most for your returns. Study after study shows that most actively managed funds fail to beat a simple market index over a long period. When you own an index fund you effectively own a small slice of hundreds of companies, which spreads out your risk. The best strategy for most people is to automate monthly contributions into a low-cost index fund and then leave it alone. Trying to time the market usually hurts your returns more than it helps. Compounding does the heavy lifting if you stay invested for decades rather than trading in and out. So the takeaway is simple: buy a broad low-cost index fund, contribute regularly, and hold for the long term. That is honestly most of what you need to know to start investing. Thanks for watching.
